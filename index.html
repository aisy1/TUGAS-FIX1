Chemical and Covalent Bond Theory

Understanding the fundamental forces that hold atoms together

üîó Chemical Bonds Overview

Chemical bonds are attractive forces that hold atoms together in compounds. They form when atoms share, transfer, or delocalize electrons to achieve stable electron configurations.

Types of Chemical Bonds:

  • Ionic Bonds - electron transfer
  • Covalent Bonds - electron sharing
  • Metallic Bonds - electron delocalization

‚öõÔ∏è Covalent Bonds

Covalent bonds form when atoms share electrons to achieve stable electron configurations. This typically occurs between nonmetal atoms with similar electronegativity values.

Characteristics:

  • Electron sharing between atoms
  • Directional bonds
  • Form discrete molecules
  • Variable bond strength

‚ö° Bond Properties

Covalent bond properties depend on the atoms involved and the number of shared electron pairs:

Bond Length: Distance between bonded nuclei (decreases with multiple bonds)

Bond Energy: Energy required to break the bond (increases with multiple bonds)

Bond Polarity: Unequal sharing due to electronegativity differences
